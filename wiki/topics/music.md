# Music Practice And Tools

The music queue is a small practice and tooling shelf. It covers singing,
guitar and piano repertoire, QWERTY music-controller ideas, DAW exploration,
and a small fiction seed involving piano as communication.

It is not currently a named project. Treat it as a topic until music becomes a
serious Conjuration, Whetstone, or Genesis thread.

## Voice Practice

The singing notes center on high-note coordination, resonance, twang, mouth
shape, larynx position, and air control. The useful practice frame is:

- reduce excess airflow on high notes;
- experiment with larynx position and twang without forcing volume;
- notice where resonance feels located: back of mouth, top of mouth, nasal
  brightness, or chest-like depth;
- use a guide, recording, or teacher when sensation labels become ambiguous;
- keep Complete Vocal Technique as a reference vocabulary, not a self-diagnosis
  trap.

CVT's four-mode vocabulary gives a rough map:

- neutral: lighter, softer, often breathier;
- curbing: contained and controlled;
- overdrive: strong, calling-like;
- edge: bright, metallic, high-intensity.

For now, the value is vocabulary and safer experimentation, not mastery of the
system from notes alone.

## Instrument And Song Practice

The guitar note is a practical repertoire list. Use it as a song-practice
queue, not as a curriculum.

Good session shape:

1. Pick one song.
2. Choose one target: chord transitions, strumming, vocal line, rhythm, or
   sing-and-play coordination.
3. Slow it down until errors are visible.
4. Record one short pass.
5. Note the next bottleneck.

The piano-talk note is a fiction seed, not a practice program: children in
strict households communicating through piano pieces. Keep it under Chronicle
or Conjuration if it becomes a scene.

## QWERTY Music Controller

The QWERTY mapping idea belongs on the boundary of music practice and Genesis
tooling.

The practical insight is not to emulate concert piano. The better target is a
computer-keyboard controller with theory primitives:

- isomorphic grid layouts for transposable chord shapes;
- scale-locked melody mode;
- one-key chord or voicing pads;
- modifiers for inversion, seventh/ninth, sus, octave, or velocity;
- arpeggiator or strum helpers for expressivity;
- polyphony-aware voicings that stay within realistic keyboard rollover.

This becomes a Genesis candidate only if it turns into an actual MIDI or
browser instrument prototype.

## DAW And Synthesis Shelf

The DAW notes point at production before a major piano purchase. Useful
explorations:

- DAW basics and arrangement;
- programmable DAW or audio tooling;
- JUCE and Tracktion Engine as possible development frameworks;
- vocal-synthesis experiments such as changing vocal register;
- sound synthesis by recreating a synth patch from a song;
- live coding as a creative-programming bridge.

The rule is to choose one tiny output: a loop, patch recreation, chord tool,
or controller prototype. Otherwise the topic becomes a resource collection.
