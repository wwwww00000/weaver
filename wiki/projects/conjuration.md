# Conjuration

Conjuration is the project for imagination building, creativity, drawing, and
the practice of making imagined worlds or forms feel available enough to bring
onto the page.

The current project center is not generic art improvement. It is the bridge
between inner visualization and external marks: constructive drawing,
perspective intuition, imagined-scene practice, and creative constraints that
make visual imagination trainable.

## Map

- [Visualization And Imagination Training](conjuration/visualization-and-imagination-training.md):
  mental imagery modes, immersive first-person visualization, image-to-page
  translation, liminal imagery capture, and short exercises for rebuilding
  imaginative vividness.
- [Constructive Drawing Practice](conjuration/constructive-drawing-practice.md):
  form construction, perspective, previsualization, reference study, line
  quality, and feedback loops with observation or 3D tools.
- [Sourcebook Worldwork](conjuration/sourcebook-worldwork.md): solo sourcebook
  tourism, lore-as-travel-guide practice, oracles, journaling, and party-based
  imaginative writing.

## Current Shape

The strongest current thread is a constrained approach to visual imagination.
The weekly note suggests turning Conjuration into a real project, recording the
original approach and past attempts, and perhaps narrowing the practice around
Pokemon-like construction exercises, think-tags-centric visualization
sketching, manga study, and scene-embedded constructive sketching.

That narrowing is useful. Conjuration should not become a vague wish to be
more creative. A good Conjuration session should have a concrete constraint:
construct a creature from primitives, imagine an object in the current visual
field, map a first-person scene onto the page, remix a sourcebook location, or
draw an invented subject with a checkable reference or model afterward.
Dream and hypnagogic fragments can be used as source material when they are
captured lightly and do not interfere with sleep.

## Practice Loops

Three loops recur across the sources.

The visualization loop starts internally: observe or imagine a scene, stabilize
the image, move viewpoint or scale, then capture just enough on paper to test
what was actually seen.

The constructive drawing loop starts with structure: choose a form, simplify it
into primitives, previsualize the line, ghost the mark, draw, then check
proportion, perspective, and form against reference or a 3D model.

The worldwork loop starts with constraints: read or choose source material,
extract places, factions, sensory anchors, mysteries, and characters, then use
light randomness or journaling to explore a scene without fully authoring the
outcome in advance.

## Boundaries

Use [Chronicle](chronicle.md) for journaling, voice, creative output, and the
life-practice layer around writing.

Use [Whetstone](whetstone.md) for metacognitive techniques, attention,
learning systems, and general cognition practice. Conjuration can borrow
think tags when they help with visualization or drawing, but the project should
stay anchored to imagination and visual creation.

Use Genesis when the material becomes a software tool for drawing,
visualization, or AI-assisted creative workflows.
