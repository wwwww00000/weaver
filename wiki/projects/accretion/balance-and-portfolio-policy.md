# Balance And Portfolio Policy

Accretion needs a written policy more than it needs isolated market views. The
core problem is not finding a clever product; it is deciding which money is
allowed to take which kind of risk.

The source balance note already points toward a useful bucket vocabulary:
liquid, allocated safe, and allocatable. The portfolio-framework source expands
that into a more explicit policy: liquidity and safety for near-term
liabilities, core growth for long-horizon wealth, and a small satellite sleeve
for active or edge-seeking strategies.

## Buckets

Use buckets before instruments.

Liquidity exists to meet known obligations without selling risk assets at a
bad time. House-related money or other time-bound liabilities should live here.

Allocated safe assets exist to preserve capital and earn a conservative return.
This can include cash-equivalent or government-backed instruments, fixed
deposits, safe bank allocations, and similar holdings. The precise instruments
will change; the role should not.

Core growth is for long-horizon compounding. It should be boring, diversified,
and governed by a rule rather than by headlines.

Satellite or edge capital is for options, structured products, single-name
views, active trades, and experiments. This sleeve needs explicit size limits
and should never borrow from liquidity or core policy after losses.

## Investment Policy Statement

A minimal investment policy statement should define:

- goals and liabilities;
- target bucket sizes;
- allowed instruments for each bucket;
- rebalancing rules;
- maximum satellite allocation;
- conditions for pausing active experiments;
- review cadence.

This document exists to prevent decision-making from being rewritten by recent
market experience. If an allocation is only acceptable when markets feel calm,
it should not be in the policy.

## Behavioral Guardrails

The sources contain a recurring behavioral problem: good or bad recent market
calls can cause later paralysis. The cure is precommitment.

- Use calendar reviews rather than headline reviews.
- Automate boring core contributions where possible.
- Separate professional trading instincts from personal wealth management.
- Make active trades small enough that they cannot impair housing or life
  plans.
- Record decisions in a way that future review can distinguish process quality
  from outcome luck.

## Current Operational Layer

The weekly note lists practical work around transfers, cards, brokerage
purchases, safe allocation, and possible housing tooling. These belong in the
task system or raw notes, not as permanent wiki conclusions.

The durable Accretion question is how those tasks map into policy: which bucket
receives the transfer, which payment-card decision changes reward efficiency,
which purchases affect safe allocation, and which software project would
reduce repeated manual work.

## Open Questions

- What exact bucket labels should be used going forward?
- What liabilities and time horizons should constrain the liquidity bucket?
- What is the target size of the satellite sleeve?
- Should core growth be automated now, or deferred until housing uncertainty is
  clearer?
- What review cadence is frequent enough for control but not so frequent that
  it encourages tinkering?
