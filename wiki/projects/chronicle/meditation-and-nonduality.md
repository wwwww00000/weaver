# Meditation And Nonduality

Chronicle uses meditation to cultivate awareness, reduce rumination, preserve
inner continuity, and create a quieter channel for experience to become
available. The point is not to collect exotic states. The point is to practice
showing up, noticing experience directly, and returning without drama.

Nondual practice sits inside that frame. Treat it as a set of phenomenological
pointers for looking at experience, not as a belief system that must be
defended.

## Consistency First

The first meditation goal is habit reliability.

- Start with 5 minutes.
- Attach the sit to a stable cue: after waking, before sleep, after shower, or
  before the first work block.
- Use a simple seat: chair, cushion, or any posture that is alert and
  sustainable.
- Track only whether the sit happened.
- Missed days should trigger a restart, not a self-judgment loop.
- Guided sits, groups, or a teacher can be used as scaffolding when the habit
  keeps failing alone.

The Chronicle minimum is a small daily awareness contact. Longer sessions,
retreats, and specialized practices are optional expansions.

## Basic Sit

A useful default session:

1. Sit upright and relax unnecessary tension.
2. Take a few ordinary breaths.
3. Put attention on one object: breath, body sensation, sound, or open visual
   field.
4. When thinking pulls attention away, notice it and return.
5. End by naming one state signal or one value for the day.

This is enough to train the main move: noticing without immediately becoming
the thought, mood, or story.

## Concentration Practice

The jhana sources are best treated as a concentration map, not as a promise.
The practical thread is:

1. Stabilize posture and object.
2. Keep returning to the object until distractions become less sticky.
3. Notice subtle breath or body sensation without forcing it.
4. If pleasantness arises, attend gently to the pleasant quality.
5. Let absorption deepen naturally instead of chasing it.

The relevant hindrances are ordinary: desire, aversion, dullness, restlessness,
and doubt. Chronicle can record them as state signals. Whetstone can treat them
as attention-control failure modes.

## Nondual Inquiry

The nondual notes point to a direct inquiry:

- Notice thoughts, sounds, sensations, and visual experience.
- Ask where the observer is.
- Look for the one who is watching.
- Notice that sights, sounds, feelings, and thoughts appear without needing a
  separate located watcher.
- Rest in the field of experience when that framing becomes available.

The "no head" and peripheral-vision pointers are useful because they interrupt
the habitual feeling of a narrow observer behind the face. Broad visual field,
raw sensation, and direct hearing can make experience feel less centered on a
solid internal narrator.

This should stay practical. If the inquiry becomes conceptual debate, return to
the basic sit.

## Portable Practice

Meditation should not depend on ideal mornings.

- Walk without audio and feel the body moving.
- Do one chore with full sensory attention.
- Take three breaths before opening a feed or work tool.
- Use a lunch or shutdown reflection when morning pages fail.
- Set a workday reflection block for higher-order planning.
- Use a short voice memo when writing would add friction.

These are Chronicle practices when they preserve awareness and continuity. They
become Whetstone practices when they are used as explicit state-control
operators.

## Liminal Capture

Several sources point at hypnagogic, dream, shower, nap, and bedtime cognition:
states where attention loosens and useful associations appear.

Use a bounded protocol:

1. Load the problem gently before rest, shower, walk, or sleep.
2. Stop active forcing.
3. Let images, fragments, or solution hints appear.
4. Capture a small handle immediately afterward.
5. Review later, when fully awake, before trusting the insight.

The rule is to protect sleep quality. Bedtime ideation is useful only if it
does not turn into insomnia or compulsive problem solving.

## Lucid Dreaming Boundary

Lucid dreaming belongs in Chronicle only as a liminal-awareness practice and in
Conjuration only when it supplies imagery or creative material.

Useful methods include:

- dream journaling;
- reality checks;
- noticing recurring dream signs;
- setting an intention before sleep;
- wake-back-to-bed or wake-initiated methods when they do not damage sleep.

This is optional. It should not compete with the core practice of ordinary
awareness during waking life.

## Rituals

Rituals can be treated as state primers: a repeated sequence that signals a
mode shift.

Examples:

- meditation seat, timer, and first breath;
- music before drawing or writing;
- shutdown review at the end of work;
- a candle, walk, or tea as a transition into reflection;
- a brief phrase before practice: "notice, soften, return."

The value of a ritual is behavioral consistency. If the ritual becomes
elaborate enough to block practice, simplify it.

## Review Loop

A weekly Chronicle review can ask:

- Did the practice happen?
- Which state appeared most often?
- Which hindrance was recurring?
- Did practice reduce rumination or restore inner voice?
- Did any liminal insight survive later review?
- Should the routine be shorter, earlier, later, guided, or more social?

The review should reward contact with experience, not dramatic meditation
stories.

- What is the smallest meditation routine that actually sticks?
- Should the practice be morning, evening, after shower, or workday transition?
- Which nondual pointer is most practical: observer search, broad vision,
  sound, body sensation, or open awareness?
- How much liminal capture is useful before it starts interfering with sleep?
- Should meditation notes remain private logs or produce distilled wiki
  practice notes?
