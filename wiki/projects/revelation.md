# Revelation

Revelation is the project for math and physics learning: beauty, mystery,
conceptual structure, and enough technical fluency to touch real questions
without disappearing into prerequisite sprawl.

The project is not a generic bookshelf. Its current shape is a constrained
learning program: a quantum mechanics spine, a differential geometry satellite,
and playground questions around measurement, emergence, renormalization, and
alternative physical laws.

## Map

- [Quantum Mechanics Spine](revelation/quantum-mechanics-spine.md): the
  Sakurai/Shankar-centered study path, differential geometry satellite, and
  measurement-problem playground.
- [Emergence And Reverse Physics](revelation/emergence-and-reverse-physics.md):
  macro-from-micro questions, renormalization group, generalized theory spaces,
  cellular automata, and toy-universe thinking.
- [Active Learning For Math And Physics](revelation/active-learning-for-math-and-physics.md):
  exercises, problem-heavy texts, just-in-time prerequisites, fatigue
  management, and study-session structure.

## Current Shape

The current plan is intentionally narrow:

- spine: quantum mechanics, slow and deep;
- satellite: differential geometry and forms, used as a language upgrade;
- playground: toy quantum systems, measurement/decoherence, renormalization
  group, and reverse-physics questions.

The source notes emphasize that learning should be active. Reading beautiful
texts is useful, but Revelation should not become spectator-mode appreciation.
Each learning block should produce a derivation, exercise solution, toy model,
synthesis note, or prerequisite scratchpad entry.

## Boundaries

Use [Genesis](genesis.md) when the material becomes AI research, coding tools,
or software for learning and exploration.

Use [P12n](p12n.md) and [Quant](../topics/quant.md) when the math is mainly in
service of trading, statistical modeling, regression, optimization, or time
series.

Use [Whetstone](whetstone.md) for general cognition and learning practice.
Revelation can reuse Whetstone methods, but the durable content here should be
math and physics structure.

## Open Questions

- What is the first 8 to 12 week Revelation sprint that is ambitious but
  survivable?
- Should the first spine be Sakurai with Shankar as support, or Shankar with
  Sakurai as structural compass?
- What toy quantum systems should be worked through first?
- Should renormalization group be treated as a near-term playground, or deferred
  until more statistical mechanics is in place?
- How should AI assistance be used without turning exercises into passive
  solution-reading?
