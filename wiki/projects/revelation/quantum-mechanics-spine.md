# Quantum Mechanics Spine

The current Revelation spine is quantum mechanics, with differential geometry
as a satellite and measurement as the first conceptual playground.

The reason to begin here is not that quantum mechanics is the easiest topic. It
is that it touches most of the project motivations at once: beautiful
structure, real mystery, transferable operator language, and enough concrete
toy systems to avoid purely philosophical drift.

## Spine, Satellite, Playground

Use one primary spine, one satellite, and one playground at a time.

The spine is quantum mechanics. It should be read slowly, with derivations and
exercises, not as a historical survey.

The satellite is differential geometry, especially differential forms,
symmetry, invariance, curvature, and variational principles. It should remain a
language upgrade, not a separate conquest.

The playground is where the project stays alive: spin systems, measurement,
decoherence, toy Hamiltonians, quantum information probes, and small
calculations that make the conceptual questions concrete.

## Text Roles

The current source-backed text comparison suggests a role split rather than a
single winner.

Sakurai is the structural compass. It foregrounds states, observables,
generators, symmetry, angular momentum, and the formal postulates in a way that
connects naturally to later physics.

Shankar is the stabilizer. It is better for patient ramp-up and continuity when
Sakurai becomes too compressed or abstract.

Zettili is a worked-example support text. It is useful for exercises and
calculation confidence, but should not define the worldview.

Feynman and path-integral material are intuition engines. Use them selectively
when the question is process, classical limit, or alternative formulation.

Information-first quantum theory is a probe. It is useful for measurement and
operational questions, but can become too abstract if used before concrete
systems are familiar.

Quantum-theory-for-mathematicians style texts are references for consistency
and rigor, not the first learning spine.

## Measurement Thread

The measurement problem is the first durable mystery.

The working distinction is:

- unitary evolution describes isolated quantum systems;
- textbook collapse describes definite measurement outcomes;
- decoherence explains why interference between macroscopic alternatives
  becomes practically unavailable;
- decoherence alone does not pick a unique experienced outcome without an
  interpretive story.

Schrodinger's cat is useful because it clarifies that opening the box is not
the magical moment. The cat, device, air, radiation, and environment have
already become entangled long before a human looks. The observer discovers an
effectively classical branch; the harder question is what branch selection
means.

## Study Loop

For each section:

1. Rewrite the primitive objects in plain language.
2. Derive at least one result by hand.
3. Do at least one exercise, or create an equivalent toy problem.
4. Add missing prerequisites to a scratchpad instead of stopping the whole
   project.
5. Write a short synthesis note when a concept changes the worldview.

AI assistance should supply hints, alternate derivations, and checks. It should
not replace first-pass struggle through the exercise.

## Open Questions

- Should the actual first pass be Sakurai-first or Shankar-first?
- Which finite-dimensional toy systems best expose measurement, noncommuting
  observables, and decoherence?
- When should differential geometry enter: symmetry/generators now, or forms
  and variational principles later?
- What counts as enough mastery to move from QM to GR, QFT, or RG?
