# Whetstone

Whetstone is the project for sharpening cognition: metacognition,
problem-solving control, thought cadence, felt-sense calibration, learning
systems, and the practices that make thinking more deliberate without making it
brittle.

The project should train the controller, not only the solver. The recurring
pattern is to notice the current cognitive state, choose a better operator,
execute for a short interval, and review what changed.

## Current Shape

Whetstone is strongest when it turns vague self-improvement into drills with
short feedback loops. The current source cluster points to four usable
directions:

- micro-tagging mental processes without breaking flow;
- decomposing problem solving into operators, drills, and transfer checks;
- using cadence and mental pages to stabilize attention without paper;
- developing felt-sense labels for expertise and inner-state recognition;
- testing cognitive-training exercises with independent outcome checks.

The imaginative language in the source notes, such as secret abilities or
domain expansion, is useful as motivation. The wiki should translate it into
repeatable practices: tags, checkpoints, representations, calibration logs,
and review rituals.

## Boundary With Chronicle

Use [Chronicle](chronicle.md) for journaling, mindfulness, rumination,
creative writing, and life review.

Use Whetstone for transferable cognitive machinery: attention control,
metacognitive monitoring, problem-solving operators, training drills, and
felt-sense calibration. The same practice may appear in both projects, but its
purpose changes by context.

## Open Questions

- Which Whetstone drills have measurable transfer instead of just feeling good?
- What is the right cadence for deliberate metacognitive practice without
  becoming self-conscious during deep work?
- Which domains should supply calibration tasks first: math, trading research,
  writing, or software building?
- How should cognitive-training experiments be tracked so they do not become
  another unsourced self-improvement narrative?
